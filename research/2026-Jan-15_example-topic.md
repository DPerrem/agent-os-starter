## TL;DR

Three to five sentences. The headline answer to the question that prompted the research. If the reader stops here, they have the answer.

For our example: *Mid-market manufacturers in the $5-50M range are spending 2-3% of revenue on operational software, up from 1% five years ago. The biggest spend categories are MRP/ERP (40%), scheduling and capacity planning (20%), and quality / compliance (15%). Buying decisions are increasingly driven by ops directors rather than CIOs, with 60-90 day evaluation cycles being typical.*

## Question

The exact question that triggered this research. Pinned at the top so future-you can see what was being asked.

> *"How are mid-market manufacturers buying operational software in 2026, and what's their typical budget?"*

---

## Method

How the research was conducted. Sources, time spent, what was excluded.

- 8 industry reports (linked below).
- 3 LinkedIn searches for "Operations Director" + "manufacturing" + recent posts.
- 2 podcasts (Manufacturing Talk, Ops Inside).
- ~3 hours total.
- **Excluded:** enterprise (>$500M revenue) and pure-software companies.

---

## Findings

### Finding 1: Spend has roughly doubled in 5 years

Citation 1 reports 2.4% of revenue average for the segment in 2024, up from 1.1% in 2019. Citation 2 corroborates with a 2.7% figure in 2025.

### Finding 2: Buying decisions have shifted from CIO to Ops Director

Three of the eight reports specifically note this shift. Reasoning: cloud-first tools don't require IT involvement to deploy, and ops directors have closer ground truth on what's actually broken.

**Implication for us:** Marketing should target ops directors directly, not IT.

### Finding 3: Evaluation cycles are 60-90 days

Shorter than enterprise (6-12 months), longer than SMB (2-4 weeks). Pattern: 30 days for evaluation, 30 days for procurement/legal, 30 days for kickoff.

**Implication for us:** Our 6-week sprint fits inside their typical adoption window.

### Finding 4: Top concerns when buying

In order: integration with existing systems (62%), cost (58%), team adoption (54%), vendor lock-in (41%). Source: citation 4.

## Implications / Recommendations

1. Update sales messaging to lead with "we work directly with Ops Directors, no IT involvement needed in week 1."
2. Update the proposal template to include an integration-and-adoption section — it's the #1 buyer concern.
3. Build a one-page market sizing summary for prospects who ask about benchmarks.
4. Schedule a follow-up research session in 6 months on AI tool adoption specifically.
