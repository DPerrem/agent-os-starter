# Proposal — [Client Name]

> Reusable proposal template. Copy, fill in, save the working draft to `~/Owner's Inbox/[YYYY-Mon-DD]_proposal_[client].md`. Final version exports to PDF.

---

## Cover

**Prepared for:** [Client name]
**Prepared by:** [Your name / company]
**Date:** [YYYY-Mon-DD]
**Valid until:** [YYYY-Mon-DD — typically 30 days]

---

## Summary

One paragraph. What you're proposing, what it costs, what it delivers, when it starts. Should be readable in 30 seconds.

---

## What We Heard

A short section reflecting back what the client told you in discovery. Three to five bullets.

This builds confidence — they see you listened. It also surfaces any misunderstanding *before* the work starts.

- [Pain point in their words]
- [Goal in their words]
- [Constraint they mentioned]

---

## What We Recommend

The core of the proposal. What you'll do, structured by phase or workstream.

### Phase 1: [Name]
- **Outcome:** [What's true at the end of this phase]
- **What we'll do:** [Concrete activities]
- **What you'll do:** [What's needed from the client]
- **Duration:** [Time frame]

### Phase 2: [Name]
[Same structure]

### Phase 3: [Name]
[Same structure]

## Deliverables

- [ ] [Deliverable 1]

## Timeline

Visual or table form. Calendar dates if known, otherwise weeks from kickoff.

| Week | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Kickoff complete |
| 2 | [Milestone] |
| ... | ... |

---

## Investment

Be clear, not coy. State the number, what's included, what's not, and payment terms.

- **Total:** [$X,XXX] [+ tax if applicable]
- **Includes:** [List]
- **Does not include:** [List anything that might be assumed but isn't]
- **Payment terms:** [e.g. 50% on signing, 50% on completion]
- **Out-of-scope work:** [Charged at $X/hour, by prior agreement only]

---

## Out of Scope

Explicitly call out what's NOT included. Saves arguments later.

- [Thing not included]
- [Thing not included]

---

## Assumptions

Things you're assuming are true. If they're wrong, scope or price changes.

- [Assumption]
- [Assumption]

---

## Risks

Two or three honest risks and how you'll handle them.

- **Risk:** [Description]. **Mitigation:** [What you'll do]
